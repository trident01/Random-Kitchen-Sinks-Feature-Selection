A Motivating Example

Consider a pathologist who has determined the tumor status (malignant or benign) for each of her patients. For each patient, she has also recorded a large set of attributes (e.g. uniformity of cell shape, average cell size, marginal adhesion), only some of which may be relevant. Our pathologist wishes to not only create a predictive model from these attributes for use on future patients, but also to interpret this model for insight into the overall problem: which attributes are most relevant to the outcome? Can she create a functional model with only a small subset of the predictors, thus potentially improving care for future patients?
